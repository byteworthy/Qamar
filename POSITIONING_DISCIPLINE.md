## What Noor IS

## Context-Specific Positioning

### App Store Descriptions

**Category**: Health & Fitness (NOT Medical)

**Short Description (80 chars):**
"Reflective companion for Muslims exploring difficult thoughts with CBT tools"

**Long Description Structure:**
1. What it offers (reflection, structure, guidance)
2. Who it's for (Muslims navigating emotional challenges)
3. What it includes (CBT-inspired tools, Islamic values)
4. What it's not (NOT therapy or religious authority)
5. When to seek professional help

**Required Disclaimers:**
- "This is not therapy, counseling, or medical treatment"
- "For crisis support, call 988"
- "Not a substitute for professional mental health care"

### Website/Marketing Copy

**Hero Headline Options:**
- "Reflection and Structure for Your Inner Work"
- "Navigate Difficult Emotions with Islamic Values"
- "A Companion for Your Emotional Wellness Journey"

**Subheadline Pattern:**
"[Action] your [emotions/thoughts] through [structure] rooted in [Islamic values]"

Example: "Explore your difficult thoughts through guided reflection rooted in mercy and hope"

**Feature Descriptions:**
- Use "helps you" not "gives you"
- Use "supports" not "treats"
- Use "guides" not "prescribes"
- Use "explores" not "diagnoses"

### Social Media

**Voice**: Warm, honest, educational

**Safe Post Types:**
- Educational content about CBT concepts
- Islamic perspective on emotional wellness
- User testimonials (with permission)
- Mental health awareness content
- Self-care tips and reflections

**Forbidden Post Types:**
- Claims of therapeutic efficacy
- Religious rulings or advice
- Promises of healing
- Comparison to professional care
- Medical advice

### Press/Media

**Elevator Pitch:**
"Noor is a mobile app that helps Muslims navigate difficult emotions through structured self-reflection inspired by CBT and rooted in Islamic values. It's not therapy‚Äîit's a companion for self-discovery."

**Key Interview Points:**

## Crisis Communication

### If Harm Occurs

**Communication Principles:**
1. **Immediate**: Acknowledge concern
2. **Truthful**: State facts without defensiveness
3. **Responsible**: Take ownership of issues
4. **Corrective**: Explain what's being fixed
5. **Humble**: No minimizing or excusing

**Never Say:**
- "This is just a tool" (sounds dismissive)
- "Users should have known" (sounds blaming)
- "We're not liable" (sounds callous)

**Do Say:**
- "We take this seriously"
- "Here's what we're doing to address this"
- "User safety is our highest priority"

### Media Crisis

**Spokesperson Rules:**
1. Only designated speakers talk to press
2. Stay on approved message points
3. Never speculate or ad-lib
4. Admit what we don't know
5. Circle back with facts

## Regulatory Considerations

### FDA/Medical Device

**Current Status**: Not a medical device
**Maintaining Status**: Never make therapeutic claims

**Triggers for Medical Device Classification:**
- Claims to diagnose, treat, cure, prevent disease
- Claims to replace medical treatment
- Marketing for medical use

**Safe Harbor**: Position as general wellness tool

### FTC Truth in Advertising

**Requirements:**
- All claims must be substantiated
- No deceptive or misleading statements
- Clear and conspicuous disclaimers
- Endorsements must be genuine

**Our Compliance:**
- No efficacy claims without evidence
- Clear disclaimers on limitations
- Authentic user testimonials only
- Honest about what product does/doesn't do

### State Counseling/Therapy Laws

**Variation**: Each state defines "practice of psychology" differently

**Our Protection:**
- Never use therapy/counseling language
- No live human counselor claims
- Clear AI/automated tool disclosure
- Not positioning as mental health service
